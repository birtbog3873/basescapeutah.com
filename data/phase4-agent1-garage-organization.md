All three trades share the same fatal standalone flaw — average job sizes that cannot support in-home-selling economics:

| Trade | Standalone Avg Ticket | In-Home Selling Minimum | Gap |
|-------|----------------------|------------------------|-----|
| Epoxy/Polyaspartic Flooring | $2,000-$5,500 | $8,000+ | -$2,500 to -$6,000 |

**Why $8K+ minimum:** In-home selling requires appointment setters ($15-20/set appointment), sales consultants ($500-$1,500 commission per close), marketing spend (15-20% of revenue), and operations overhead. At $3,000 average ticket with 40% close rate, CAC alone consumes 25-30% of revenue before materials or labor.

#### Customer Complaints (Differentiation Opportunities)

Based on review analysis across Google, Yelp, BBB, and industry forums:

1. **Hot tire pickup** — The #1 complaint for water-based epoxy. Polyaspartic solves this, but customers don't know the difference. *Opportunity: Education-based selling ("We use polyaspartic, not epoxy — here's why your neighbor's floor is peeling").*
2. **Peeling/delamination** — Caused by inadequate surface prep (skipped grinding, moisture testing). *Opportunity: Documented prep process with before/after photos, moisture testing guarantee.*
3. **Slipperiness when wet** — Especially in Utah winters with snow/salt tracking. *Opportunity: Anti-slip aggregate standard in all installs, not a paid add-on.*
4. **UV yellowing** — Epoxy yellows, polyaspartic doesn't. *Opportunity: Polyaspartic-only positioning with UV-stability guarantee.*
5. **Scheduling/wait times** — Several Utah competitors have long lead times due to high demand. *Opportunity: Fast scheduling (7-day or less turnaround).*

#### In-Home Selling Model Assessment

GarageExperts and Garage Force both use free on-site estimates, but neither runs a dedicated appointment-setter → design consultant → one-call-close model for flooring alone. The ticket size doesn't justify it. Most use a simpler estimator model (1 person measures, quotes, follows up). **Nobody is running the full Bath Fitter / Re-Bath style in-home sales machine for garage floors alone.**

#### Market Gaps

- No competitor positions on "polyaspartic ONLY" (most offer both epoxy and polyaspartic)
- No competitor offers a meaningful warranty beyond "lifetime" vagueness — no money-back satisfaction guarantee
- No competitor bundles floor coating with organization into a single branded package with one-day turnaround
- No competitor uses 3D rendering/visualization in the sales process for garage floors

#### Customer Complaints (Differentiation Opportunities)

1. **Franchise pricing markup** — Customers consistently complain about high prices from franchise companies. Smart Space Design already positions against this ("no franchise markup"). *Limited opportunity: price-based positioning is a race to the bottom.*

5. **High-pressure sales tactics** — Some franchise models use aggressive one-call-close tactics. *Opportunity: "No-pressure" positioning with written quote valid for 30 days.*

#### In-Home Selling Model Assessment

**This IS the franchise playbook.** Closets by Design literally invented the in-home closet design consultation model in 1982. Every major franchise competitor runs this exact model. An independent startup would be entering the market running the same playbook as 5+ established national brands with decades of consumer awareness.

**Structural disadvantage:** The franchises have manufacturing infrastructure (Closets by Design manufactures in-house, Closet Factory has 24 years of SLC manufacturing). An independent using component-based systems (melamine panels from wholesale suppliers) cannot match the customization level of these manufacturers.

#### Market Gaps

Honestly, few. This market is thoroughly served by both franchise and independent operators. The only potential gap:
- Bundled closet + garage organization from a single provider (most closet companies don't do garage floors, and vice versa)
- Ultra-fast turnaround using component-based systems vs. custom manufacturing
- Aggressive post-move-in targeting of new homeowners
