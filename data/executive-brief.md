# Executive Brief: Contractor Trade Evaluation

**Prepared by Steven Bunker, AchieveCMO | March 2026**

---

## Purpose

This document summarizes a systematic evaluation of 1,200+ contractor service entries to identify the best trade opportunity for a direct-to-residential, in-home-selling startup on the Wasatch Front, Utah. The goal: a $500K-$1M Year 1 business launched with under $100K in capital.

---

## Evaluation Pipeline

| Phase | What It Did | Input | Output |
|-------|------------|-------|--------|
| Phase 1: List Building | Normalized and deduplicated 1,350 raw entries from 7 sources into a master list | 7 reference lists (Angi, Thumbtack, HomeAdvisor, Houzz, etc.) | 93 unique trades |
| Phase 2: First Pass Screening | Scored each trade against a 16-dimension weighted rubric (max 410 points) | 38 trades scored (29 main + 7 reconsidered + 2 calibration anchors) | 27 in Consideration |
| Phase 3: Research Validation | Validated scores with 400+ web searches across 5 areas per trade | 27 trades researched | Top 10 ranked |
| Phase 4: Deep Analysis | Full competitive analysis, revenue modeling, go-to-market planning | 13 trades deep-analyzed | 2 recommended paths |

---

## Scoring System

The 16-dimension scoring rubric (max 410 points) uses weighted dimensions to evaluate each trade against the in-home-selling business model. The "Big 4" sub-score (max 175 points) captures the four most critical dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | Why It Matters |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Competition Level | 10 | Fewer competitors = easier market entry |
| Average Job Size | 9 | Must support in-home-selling economics ($15K-$25K sweet spot) |
| Differentiation Potential | 8 | Ability to create a branded offer that stands apart |
| Gross Margin | 8 | Must cover salesperson commissions + marketing + overhead |

**Phase 3 cutoffs:** Total 290+ AND Big 4 122+ to advance.

---

## Key Finding: The Recurring Tension

Trades with the best operational profiles (fast installs, high margins, small crews) have small tickets. Trades with sweet-spot tickets ($15K+) have operational complexity. This tension is structural, not an artifact of the scoring.

The solution: **bundling** — combining operationally simple trades into a single package that hits the ticket sweet spot.

## The Two Viable Paths

### Path A: "Total Garage Transformation" (Lower Risk, Faster Ramp)

Bundled polyaspartic garage flooring + organization (cabinets, slatwall, overhead racks). A proven franchise playbook executed without franchise fees or royalties.

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Average Ticket | $11,000-$13,000 (blended bundle) |
| Gross Margin | 55-60% |
| Crew | 2-person, cross-trained for floor + storage |
| Install Time | 2-3 days |
| Licensing | S300 + S220 (25-hour courses, no experience required) |
| Seasonality | Year-round |
| Startup Capital | $67K-$103K |
| Competitors | 4+ franchises (GarageExperts, PremierGarage, Garage Living) |
| Competitive Moat | Operational — speed, no franchise markup, polyaspartic-only |

**Revenue Projections:**

| Scenario | Year 1 Revenue | Year 1 Gross Profit |
|----------|---------------|---------------------|
| Conservative | $720,000 | $396,000 |
| Base | $1,080,000 | $594,000 |
| Aggressive | $1,440,000 | $792,000 |

**Bundle Economics:** The bundle generates 3.4x the net margin of standalone flooring while using the same lead, same sales visit, same crew, and same truck. A standalone $4,500 floor job produces $1,518 in net margin. The $11,500 bundle produces $5,118 from the same appointment.

**Why it works:** Revenue from Week 2. Proven playbook (GarageExperts FDD confirms ~48% gross profit). Year-round cash flow. Expansion into closets, mudroom, and outdoor concrete uses the same skills and equipment.

**Key risk:** Competing against established franchises with brand recognition and marketing budgets. Differentiation is real but incremental, not category-creating.

### Path B: "WalkoutPro — Utah's Basement Income Specialists" (Wider Moat, Higher Ceiling)

Basement walkout/ADU specialist leveraging Steven's established construction relationships.

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Average Ticket | $35,000-$55,000 |
| Gross Margin | 40-50% |
| Gross Profit Per Job | $16,000-$25,000 |
| Crew | 2-3 in-house + subs (BullRock excavation, framing companies) |
| Install Time | Multi-week per project |
| Licensing | GC license or qualifier required |
| Seasonality | 9 months (March-November) |
| Startup Capital | $50K-$80K + partner investment |
| Competitors | Near-zero (1 dedicated specialist in all of Utah) |
| Competitive Moat | Structural — category creation, no competitors |

**Revenue Projections:**

| Scenario | Year 1 Revenue | Year 1 Gross Profit |
|----------|---------------|---------------------|
| Conservative | $405,000 | $162,000 |
| Base | $720,000 | $324,000 |
| Aggressive | $1,350,000 | $608,000 |

**Why it works:** Near-zero competition with the highest Big 4 score in the pipeline (141-149). Steven's existing relationships eliminate the three biggest operational barriers: BullRock for excavation, framing company connections for structural work, and equipment rental friends willing to invest. The ADU/rental income narrative ("What would an extra $1,500/month mean for your family?") is the strongest marketing opportunity found. Massive upsell path: walkout ($35K) to finish ($25K-$45K) to full ADU apartment ($55K-$100K+).

**Key risks:** Slower ramp (Month 2-3 for first revenue), seasonal cash flow gap (Dec-Feb), foundation structural liability, and complex permitting.

- $140K+/month revenue
- On track for $1M+ Year 1

**Path B at Month 12:**

- $70K-$100K/month revenue
- Pre-sold 2-3 spring projects before winter break
- On track for $720K+ Year 1

---

## Recommendation

**The right choice depends on founder risk appetite.**

Path A is the safer bet. Economics work even at conservative assumptions. Revenue starts in Week 2. Year-round cash flow eliminates seasonal risk. The trade-off: competing in an established category against funded franchises.

Path B has the wider moat. Near-zero competition. Category creation. The highest Big 4 score in the pipeline. Steven's construction relationships solve the operational barriers that originally disqualified the trade. The trade-off: slower ramp, seasonal gap, foundation liability, and a conservative case ($405K) that misses the $500K floor.

**Both paths are real.** This analysis provides the data to make a confident decision.
