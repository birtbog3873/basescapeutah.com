### Research Findings

**Local Competition:** Research identified 6-10 sauna installation businesses serving the Wasatch Front, including Salt Lake Custom Saunas, Custom Utah Saunas, Rogue Saunas, The Home Sauna, Lifespan Bath & Remodel, and Backyards of America. The market is fragmented with no dominant player and no franchise presence. Critically, no competitor appears to use a dedicated in-home-selling model — most operate as builder/installer shops or hot tub/spa dealers that also do saunas. Sources: [Yelp SLC Sauna Installation](https://www.yelp.com/search?cflt=saunainstallation&find_loc=Salt+Lake+City,+UT), [Angi SLC Sauna](https://www.angi.com/companylist/us/ut/salt-lake-city/sauna-installation.htm).

**Job Size & Pricing:** HomeGuide reports average sauna installation at $3,100 (range $2,500-$7,000). Infrared saunas cost $2,300-$8,500 installed; prefab units $2,300-$14,500; custom-built saunas $4,300-$34,000. Premium custom builds with full installation reach $10K-$25K+. The sweet spot for this business model would be curated premium packages in the $8K-$18K range — below the ideal $15K-$25K but achievable with premium positioning and add-ons. Sources: [HomeGuide](https://homeguide.com/costs/sauna-cost), [HomeAdvisor](https://www.homeadvisor.com/cost/swimming-pools-hot-tubs-and-saunas/install-a-sauna/), [Haven of Heat](https://havenofheat.com/blogs/sauna-guides/typical-cost-to-have-a-sauna-installed).

**Margins:** Sauna business profit margins reported at 20-30% operating (EBITDA). Installation is material-heavy: prefab sauna units represent the bulk of project cost, with labor/installation at $300-$2,500. Custom builds offer better margin potential. General contractor markup benchmarks of 20-40% apply. Gross margins likely 35-45% on turnkey packages, improving with custom/premium positioning. Sources: [Haven of Heat](https://havenofheat.com/blogs/sauna-guides/how-to-make-money-with-a-sauna-business), [Business Plan Suite](https://businessplansuite.com/blogs/owners-make/sauna).

**Local Demand & Utah Factors:** U.S. sauna market growing at 6.4% CAGR, projected to surpass $200M by 2026. Home wellness is a powerful macro trend — 35% of new high-end residential projects include wellness amenities. Utah's cold winters (6+ months) create natural demand for indoor warmth/wellness features. Fast-growing population with new construction provides installation opportunities. Smart sauna systems represent 34% of new installations. Utah's health-conscious, family-oriented culture aligns well with home wellness positioning. Sources: [Topture Sauna Industry Statistics](https://topture.com/blogs/guides/sauna-industry-statistics-trends), [Technavio US Sauna Market](https://www.technavio.com/report/us-sauna-market-analysis), [Kozi Wellness](https://www.koziwellness.com/blogs/news/home-saunas-the-rising-wellness-trend-for-2025-2026).

**Licensing & Permits:** No sauna-specific contractor license exists in Utah. Would likely operate under a general contractor or specialty contractor license through DOPL. Electrical work requires a licensed electrician for 240V circuits (most traditional sauna heaters). Electrical permits are mandatory — circuit sizing, GFCI protection, and disconnect switch inspection required. Building permits may be needed for structural modifications (indoor builds) or outdoor structures depending on municipality. Sources: [DOPL Specialty Contractor](https://dopl.utah.gov/contracting/apply-for-a-license/specialty-contractor-license/), [Haven of Heat Permits Guide](https://havenofheat.com/blogs/sauna-guides/permits-required-for-installing-a-sauna-what-you-need-to-know).

### Key Insight
> Sauna installation narrowly qualifies as a candidate (290/290, 123/122) driven by exceptional demand tailwinds and zero in-home-selling competition. The biggest challenge is job size — most installations fall in the $3K-$10K range, requiring deliberate premium positioning (custom builds, full design packages, smart integration) to push average tickets into the $12K-$18K zone needed for the business model to work at scale. The wellness trend and lack of franchise competition create a genuine first-mover opportunity on the Wasatch Front.

### Cross-Cutting Observations

1. **Competition was consistently underestimated in Phase 2.** Four of six trades had competition scores drop by 1-2 points after local market research. Franchise penetration in Utah home services is higher than heuristic screening assumed.

2. **Job size is the structural killer for this batch.** Five of six trades have typical job sizes below $8K. Only outdoor kitchens naturally hit the $15K-$25K sweet spot, but they fail on margins and operational complexity.

3. **Sauna installation is the lone survivor** and it barely qualifies (290/290, 123/122). Its case rests on (a) no in-home-selling competitor anywhere in the market, (b) exceptional demand tailwinds (6.4% CAGR, wellness macro trend), and (c) the ability to curate premium packages that push average tickets from the typical $5K-$8K into the $12K-$18K range through design consultation, smart features, and wellness positioning.

4. **Margins separate "nice businesses" from "scalable businesses."** Outdoor lighting (58%+ gross margin) has the best margin profile but can't overcome tiny job sizes and franchise saturation. Sauna installation's margins (35-45%) are workable but not exceptional — premium positioning is essential.

5. **The in-home-selling model is already deployed** in cabinet refacing (N-Hance) and custom shelving (Closets by Design, California Closets). Competing against operators who have perfected your proposed sales model over decades is a non-starter.
