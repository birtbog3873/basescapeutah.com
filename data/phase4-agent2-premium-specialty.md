**Critical finding: Zero competitors use a dedicated in-home-selling model.** Every sauna business on the Wasatch Front operates as either (a) a builder/installer shop that takes inbound requests, (b) a hot tub/spa dealer that stocks sauna units, or (c) a mobile rental business. Nobody runs appointment setters, in-home design consultants, or one-call-close sales processes.

**Customer Complaints (Differentiation Opportunities):**
Research across Yelp, BBB, and Google Reviews for sauna companies nationally reveals these recurring pain points:
- **Electrical failures and tripped breakers** — improper electrical work is the #1 complaint category. Failed heating panels, power box failures, GFCI tripping.
- **WiFi/app connectivity issues** — smart sauna features marketed but non-functional; "advertised app control that doesn't work."
- **Poor post-sale support** — unanswered emails and voicemails; automated troubleshooting with no follow-up for weeks.
- **Construction quality gaps** — cold air leaking through poorly sealed doors; shipping damage; missing components.
- **Warranty frustrations** — expensive replacements ($1,500+) just outside warranty; unclear warranty terms.

These complaints map directly to differentiation opportunities: guaranteed electrical work, verified smart integration before handoff, responsive warranty service, and sealed/inspected quality standards.

**Market Gaps:**
- No one offers a curated "home wellness suite" experience (sauna + cold plunge + outdoor shower as a package)
- No design consultation with 3D rendering or material samples brought to the home
- No financing options marketed by any local sauna installer
- No branded warranty or service guarantee beyond standard manufacturer coverage
- No one targets the luxury new-construction market with builder partnerships

**What Kills the Business in Year 1:**
The single greatest threat is **failing to establish premium positioning from day one.** If the business accepts too many $4K-$6K prefab installs to "keep busy," it trains the market (and the sales team) to sell cheap. The revenue math simply doesn't work at commodity ticket sizes. The founders must have the discipline to turn away small jobs or price them at a premium that pushes customers up to packages.

**Seasonal Cash Flow:**
Saunas are less seasonal than most outdoor trades, but there is a pattern:
- **Oct-Apr (7 months):** Peak selling season — cold weather drives "I want a sauna" impulse purchases. 60-65% of annual revenue.
- **May-Sep (5 months):** Slower but not dead — outdoor sauna and wellness suite projects sell well in summer; backyard living trend helps. 35-40% of annual revenue.
- **Cash flow implication:** Build cash reserves during Oct-Mar peak to cover May-Jul dip. Financing option for customers smooths revenue timing.
