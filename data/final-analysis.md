## Final Recommendation Narrative

### 1. Executive Summary — Two Viable Paths

This analysis produces two genuinely viable launch options, not one. The right choice depends on founder risk appetite and which competitive moat Steven wants to build.

**Path A: "Total Garage Transformation" (Lower risk, faster ramp)**
Launch a bundled polyaspartic garage flooring + organization company. Proven franchise playbook without franchise fees. $11K-$13K avg ticket, 55-60% margins, 2-person crews, 2-3 day installs, year-round. Competing against established franchises but with operational advantages.

**Path B: "WalkoutPro — Utah's Basement Income Specialists" (Wider moat, higher ceiling)**
Launch a basement walkout/ADU specialist leveraging Steven's established relationships: BullRock excavation (former partial owner), heavy equipment rental company friends (willing to invest/partner), and framing company connections. Near-zero competition (1 dedicated specialist in all of Utah). $30K-$60K jobs, 40-50% margins, Big 4 of 141-149 (highest in pipeline). ### 3. The Case for Path A (Garage Bundle)

1. **Execution certainty.** Economics work even at conservative assumptions ($720K Year 1). Proven playbook — GarageExperts FDD confirms ~48% gross profit. Revenue from Week 2.

5. **The ADU narrative is a marketing masterpiece.** "What would an extra $1,500/month mean for your family?" + ROI calculator + comparable rental data + financing as a profit center. This is the single best application of Steven's offer-building skills in the entire pipeline.
