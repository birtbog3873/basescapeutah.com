# Target Category Profile: Contractor Trade Opportunity Selection

> **Purpose:** Structured scoring rubric to evaluate candidate contractor trades for a direct-to-residential startup on the Wasatch Front, Utah. Use this profile as the measurement tool when researching and comparing specific trades.

---

## Section 1: Founder Profile

| Founder | Background |
|---------|-----------|
| **Steven Bunker** | 10+ years direct-to-consumer home services (marketing, sales ops, lead gen). Ran marketing for Western Timber Frame (pergolas, pavilions, decks). |
| **Business Partner** | Sales team management, installation crew management, field operations. |

**Combined Edge:** Full-stack in-home sales operation — marketing engine + sales management + field ops. The team can generate leads, close in-home, and manage crews from day one without outsourcing any core function.

---

## Section 2: Business Model Parameters (Non-Negotiable)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Business Model | Direct to Residential, In-Home Selling |
| Structure | Independent startup (no franchise) |
| Market | Wasatch Front, Utah (SLC, Utah County, Davis County) |
| Year 1 Revenue | $500K–$1M |
| Startup Capital | Under $100K |
| Equipment | Rentals + subcontracted labor initially |
| Facility | Home office + small warehouse (no showroom) |

## Section 3: Scoring Rubric

16 dimensions, each scored 1–5. Weighted by importance to this specific business model.

| # | Dimension | Weight | Rationale |
|---|-----------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | Competition Level | **10** | In a low-competition trade, Steven's marketing skills become a massive unfair advantage. This is the #1 leverage point. |
| 2 | Average Job Size | **9** | Drives the entire economics of in-home selling. Too low = can't afford sales team + marketing. Too high = cash deals don't work initially. |
| 3 | Differentiation Potential | **8** | Steven's core skill is offer-building. The trade must allow differentiation through value stacking, guarantees, and bundling. |
| 4 | Gross Margin | **8** | Must cover salesperson commissions (8–12%), marketing/lead gen (15–20%), overhead, and still profit. |
| 5 | Installation Time | **7** | Affects crew throughput and cash flow velocity. 2–3 days = 1–2 jobs/week/crew. |
| 6 | Post-Sale Risk | **7** | Low callbacks = lean operations, predictable costs, no distraction from sales. Critical for a startup with no service infrastructure. |
| 7 | Labor Skill Level | **6** | Easier to recruit = faster to scale. Sub labor market in Utah matters. |
| 8 | Sales Cycle Efficiency | **5** | One-visit scoping important for throughput, but flexible on the close itself. |
| 9 | Financing Compatibility | **5** | Cash deals initially, financing at scale. Trade needs to work both ways. |
| 10 | Crew Size | **4** | 2–3 person crews preferred for manageable scaling. |
| 11 | Demand Tailwind | **4** | Not required but a macro trend makes everything easier — marketing, urgency, pricing power. |
| 12 | Permits & Inspections | **3** | Not a deal-breaker but simpler = faster job cycle. |
| 13 | Upsell / Add-on Potential | **3** | Nice to have — increases average ticket without needing more leads. |
| 14 | Supply Chain Complexity | **2** | Moderate complexity OK. Only a factor if supply chain creates unpredictable delays. |
| 15 | Seasonality | **1** | Seasonal is fine. Barely weighted. |
| 16 | Visibility of Work | **0** | Explicitly not a factor. |

**Total weight points: 82 | Max possible score: 410**

---

## Section 4: Scoring Definitions

For each dimension, definitions for scores of 1, 3, and 5. Interpolate for 2 and 4.

### 1. Competition Level (Weight: 10)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | No dedicated in-home-sales competitor on Wasatch Front; mostly served by generalists as a side offering |
| **3** | 2–5 dedicated competitors, but market not saturated |
| **1** | Highly saturated (10+ competitors, established brands, franchise presence) |

### 2. Average Job Size (Weight: 9)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Sweet spot $15K–$25K (works for cash AND financed deals) |
| **3** | $10K–$15K or $25K–$30K (edges of range — either tight margins or tough cash closes) |
| **1** | Below $8K (can't support in-home sales model) or above $35K (too hard to close cash) |

### 3. Differentiation Potential (Weight: 8)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Multiple natural differentiation levers: warranty, process, materials, design, guarantee, bundling |
| **3** | Some differentiation possible but limited levers |
| **1** | Pure commodity — lowest price wins |

### 4. Gross Margin (Weight: 8)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | 55%+ gross margin achievable |
| **3** | 40–50% gross margin |
| **1** | Below 35% (can't support the sales model) |

### 5. Installation Time (Weight: 7)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | 1–2 days (high throughput, fast cash cycle) |
| **3** | 2–3 days (target range) |
| **1** | 5+ days (ties up crew, slow cash cycle) |

### 6. Post-Sale Risk (Weight: 7)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Install-and-done — virtually no callbacks if installed correctly |
| **3** | Occasional callbacks (1–5% of jobs), manageable |
| **1** | High callback/warranty burden (water intrusion, structural, mechanical systems) |

### 7. Labor Skill Level (Weight: 6)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | General labor trainable in 1–2 weeks |
| **3** | Specialized training needed (1–3 months), but not licensed trade |
| **1** | Requires licensed/certified tradespeople (electrician, plumber, HVAC) |

### 8. Sales Cycle Efficiency (Weight: 5)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Measure, scope, and price in one 60–90 min visit; close within 1 week |
| **3** | One visit to scope, but needs follow-up design/engineering before pricing |
| **1** | Multi-visit consultative process, 4+ week sales cycle |

### 9. Financing Compatibility (Weight: 5)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Job size works well as cash deal AND with consumer financing; no financing needed to launch |
| **3** | Financing helpful but not essential; homeowners can pay cash at target job size |
| **1** | Financing required for most sales (job size too high for cash), creating chicken-and-egg problem at launch |

### 10. Crew Size (Weight: 4)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | 2-person crews standard |
| **3** | 3–4 person crews |
| **1** | 5+ person crews or heavy equipment operators needed |

### 11. Demand Tailwind (Weight: 4)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Strong macro trend driving demand (legislation, climate, demographics) with measurable YoY growth |
| **3** | Steady existing demand, no decline but no acceleration |
| **1** | Declining demand or headwinds (economic sensitivity, regulatory burden) |

### 12. Permits & Inspections (Weight: 3)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | No permits required |
| **3** | Simple, predictable permit process (1–2 day turnaround) |
| **1** | Complex permitting, multiple inspections, engineering stamps required |

### 13. Upsell / Add-on Potential (Weight: 3)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | 3+ natural add-ons that can increase ticket 30–50% |
| **3** | 1–2 logical add-ons |
| **1** | Scope is fixed, no natural upsell path |

### 14. Supply Chain Complexity (Weight: 2)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | 1–2 suppliers, commodity materials, readily available |
| **3** | Specialty materials from 2–4 suppliers, 1–2 week lead times |
| **1** | Custom manufacturing, long lead times, import dependencies |

### 15. Seasonality (Weight: 1)

| Score | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **5** | Year-round work |
| **3** | 8–9 month season (Mar–Nov) |
| **1** | 5–6 month season |

### 16. Visibility of Work (Weight: 0)

Not scored — explicitly excluded as a factor.
