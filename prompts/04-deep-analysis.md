# Phase 4: Deep Analysis & Final Recommendation

> **Role:** You are a startup strategy consultant performing deep-dive analysis on the final candidate contractor trades for a direct-to-residential, in-home-selling startup on the Wasatch Front, Utah. Your analysis will directly inform the founders' decision on which trade to launch.
>
> **Input files:** `data/candidate-list.md` + `Target Category Profile.md` + `Top500 - Sheet1.csv`
> **Output file:** Save your output to `data/final-analysis.md`

---

## Your Task

For each Candidate trade (~5-10), perform comprehensive research across 8 dimensions. Produce a definitive ranking with an opinionated final recommendation.

---

## Founder Context

Keep these founder strengths top of mind — they shape "founder fit" for every trade:

- **Steven Bunker:** 10+ years direct-to-consumer home services. Marketing engine builder (lead gen, offer construction, brand positioning). Ran marketing for Western Timber Frame (pergolas, pavilions, decks). Expert at differentiation through value stacking, guarantees, and bundled offers.
- **Business Partner:** Sales team management, installation crew management, field operations. Can hire, train, and manage sales reps and install crews.
- **Combined edge:** They can generate leads, close in-home, and manage crews from day one — no outsourcing of core functions.
- **Startup capital:** Under $100K
- **Year 1 target:** $500K-$1M revenue

## Research Dimensions

For each Candidate, research all 8 dimensions below. Be thorough — this is the final analysis that drives the business decision.

### Dimension 1: Competitive Landscape (Deep)

Go beyond Phase 3's competitor count. For the Wasatch Front specifically:

- **Competitor inventory:** List all identifiable competitors by name. For each:
  - Company name and website
  - Estimated size (revenue range, employee count if available)
  - Years in business
  - Positioning (budget, mid-range, premium)
  - Business model (lead gen → estimate, showroom, in-home sales, referral only)
- **Customer complaints:** Search Google Reviews, Yelp, BBB for common complaints about existing competitors. These are differentiation opportunities.
- **In-home selling model:** Is anyone doing dedicated in-home sales (appointment setters → consultants → one-call-close)? This is THE key question. If nobody does this, Steven's team has a structural advantage.
- **Market gaps:** What are customers asking for that nobody provides?

### Dimension 2: Customer Acquisition

- **Google Ads:** Search for CPC data on "[trade] Utah," "[trade] Salt Lake City," "[trade] near me" keywords. Use Google Keyword Planner ranges or industry benchmarks.
- **Estimated CPL:** Based on CPC and typical landing page conversion rates (5-15%), estimate cost per lead.
- **Lead sources that work:** Which channels generate leads in this trade? (Google Ads, Facebook/Instagram, Nextdoor, yard signs, Home shows, door-to-door, referral programs, direct mail)
- **Close rate estimates:** What's typical for in-home sales in this trade? (Industry data or analogous trades)
- **CAC calculation:** CPL ÷ close rate = CAC. Is CAC sustainable given average job size and margin?

### Dimension 3: Supply Chain & Suppliers

- **Utah-based suppliers:** Identify specific material suppliers in Utah for this trade
- **Material cost as % of job:** What portion of the job cost is materials?
- **Lead times:** How long from order to delivery for key materials?
- **Volume discount potential:** Do suppliers offer volume pricing? At what thresholds?
- **Supply chain risks:** Single-source dependencies, import risks, price volatility

### Dimension 4: Labor & Operations

- **Crew composition:** What does a typical crew look like? (Lead installer + helper, specialized roles, etc.)
- **Training timeline:** How long to get a new crew member productive?
- **Hourly rates in Utah:** What do installers earn in this trade on the Wasatch Front?
- **Sub availability vs. W-2:** Can you sub out installation initially? Is there a sub market for this trade in Utah?
- **Equipment needs:** What equipment is needed and what does it cost? Can it be rented initially?
- **Scaling path:** What does going from 1 crew to 3 crews look like?

### Dimension 6: Differentiation Playbook

Steven's core skill is offer construction. For this trade, design:

- **Branded offer concept:** A specific, named offer that bundles the service with differentiators (e.g., "The Forever Floor System" for epoxy, "The Complete Comfort Package" for insulation). Include what's in it.
- **Warranty structure:** What warranty can be offered that competitors don't? (Length, coverage, transferability)
- **Add-on menu with pricing:** 3-5 natural add-ons with estimated price points that increase the average ticket
- **Customer experience differentiators:** What can the sales and installation process look like that's meaningfully different from competitors? (Communication, cleanliness, speed, follow-up)
- **Guarantee:** A risk-reversal guarantee that makes it easy to say yes

### Dimension 7: Risk Assessment

- **Top 3 risks** with:
  - Likelihood (Low / Medium / High)
  - Impact (Low / Medium / High)
  - Specific mitigation strategy
- **What kills the business in Year 1?** The single most dangerous scenario. Be specific.
- **Seasonal cash flow analysis:** For seasonal trades, map monthly revenue and identify cash-tight months. What fills the gap?

### Dimension 8: Go-to-Market Sketch

- **Month 1-3 (Launch):**
  - Key hires and their roles
  - Marketing channels to activate (and budget)
  - First 10 customers — where do they come from?
  - Equipment and supply setup
  - Target: first revenue by end of Month 1

- **Month 4-6 (Growth):**
  - Marketing optimization (what's working, what to cut)
  - Crew scaling plan
  - Process documentation
  - Target: steady-state jobs per month

- **Month 7-12 (Scale):**
  - Second crew addition timing and trigger
  - Referral program launch
  - Brand building activities
  - Target: on track for Year 1 revenue goal

- **First hire sequence:** In what order do you hire? (e.g., 1st: lead installer, 2nd: helper, 3rd: appointment setter, 4th: second crew lead)

- **Initial marketing spend:** Monthly budget and channel allocation

## Research Quality Rules

1. **Be specific, not generic.** "There are several competitors" is not useful. "Google Maps shows 8 companies advertising [trade] in Salt Lake County, of which 3 appear to be established businesses with 50+ reviews" is useful.

2. **No fabrication.** If you can't find data, say "No data found" and note the impact on your analysis. Do not invent competitor names, pricing data, or market statistics.

3. **Show your math.** Revenue projections should have clear inputs. CAC calculations should show the formula.

4. **Be opinionated.** This analysis is meant to drive a decision. Don't hedge everything — make a clear recommendation and defend it.

5. **Think like a founder.** Every insight should connect back to: "So what does this mean for Steven and his partner launching this business with $100K in Utah?"
