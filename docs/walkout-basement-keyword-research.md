## 1. Terminology Map

Homeowners use many different terms to describe walkout basement projects. Understanding the semantic universe is critical because Google Ads broad and phrase match will capture variations, but exact match requires precision.

| Term | What It Means | Who Uses It | Search Intent |
|------|---------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Walkout basement** | Basement with a ground-level door leading directly outside | Most common consumer term | High intent |
| **Basement walkout** | Same as above, reversed word order | Common in Utah specifically | High intent |
| **Basement walkout entrance** | The entryway/door component specifically | Homeowners researching the project | High intent |
| **Daylight basement** | Basement with full-sized windows above grade but NO exterior door | Homeowners who confuse terminology; also home buyers | Mixed — may convert if educated |
| **Garden-level basement** | Partially above grade, between standard and walkout | Real estate listings, Colorado/East Coast term | Lower intent for contractor services |
| **Lookout basement** | Regional synonym for daylight basement | Less common, mostly real estate | Low intent |
| **Basement entrance** | Generic — could mean walkout, could mean external stairway | Broad searches | Medium intent |
| **Basement exit** | Safety/egress framing | Code compliance searches | Medium intent |
| **Separate basement entrance** | ADU/rental framing — homeowners wanting to create a separate unit | Strong ADU intent | High intent (ADU angle) |
| **Basement conversion** | Broader term — could be finishing, walkout, or ADU | Broad funnel | Medium intent |
| **Basement excavation** | The construction method (digging out for walkout) | Researching the process | Medium-high intent |
| **Foundation cutting** | Cutting a door opening in an existing foundation wall | Deep research phase | High intent (specific knowledge) |
| **Egress window** | Emergency exit window required by code | Code compliance, related service | Adjacent service intent |
| **Basement apartment** / **Basement ADU** | Rental unit angle — walkout is often required for ADU compliance | Income-motivated homeowners | High intent (different motivation) |

**Key insight:** "Walkout basement" and "basement walkout" are the dominant search terms. "Daylight basement" captures a meaningful secondary audience — many searchers actually want a walkout but use the wrong term. "Basement entrance" and "separate entrance" capture ADU-motivated searchers who may not know the term "walkout."

## 3. Keyword Themes for Ad Groups

### Recommended Ad Group Structure (Walkout Basements Campaign)

```
Campaign: Search — Walkout Basements
│
├── Ad Group 1: Walkout Basement — Contractor (High Intent)
│   Keywords: contractor, builder, company, specialist, near me, hire
│   Landing: /lp/walkout-basements
│
├── Ad Group 2: Walkout Basement — Cost & Pricing
│   Keywords: cost, price, estimate, quote, how much, financing
│   Landing: /lp/walkout-basements (cost section anchor)
│
├── Ad Group 3: Walkout Basement — Conversion & Construction
│   Keywords: conversion, construction, build, add, install, excavation
│   Landing: /lp/walkout-basements
│
├── Ad Group 4: Basement Entrance & ADU (Intent Bridge)
│   Keywords: basement entrance, separate entrance, basement apartment,
│             ADU basement, basement rental unit, daylight basement
│   Landing: /lp/walkout-basements (ADU messaging variant)
│
└── Ad Group 5: Walkout Basement — Questions (Remarketing/Lower Funnel)
│   Keywords: pros and cons, permit, timeline, waterproofing, value
│   Landing: /services/walkout-basements or /blog/walkout-basement-guide
│   NOTE: Only activate if budget allows; primarily for RLSA
```

### Why This Structure

- **Ad Group 1** targets people ready to hire — highest bids, tightest match types
- **Ad Group 2** targets people comparing prices — moderate bids, messaging emphasizes transparent pricing (our differentiator vs. competitors who hide prices)
- **Ad Group 3** captures people describing what they want done vs. looking for who does it
- **Ad Group 4** captures the ADU/rental income audience who may not know the term "walkout" — this is a unique audience with different messaging (income, HB 82/398, separate entrance)
- **Ad Group 5** is for RLSA and lower-funnel remarketing only — don't spend primary budget here

- Lower CPCs than broad "basement remodeling" terms

## 8. Customer Journey & Search Behavior

### The Walkout Basement Buyer Journey

Understanding when and why homeowners search is critical for matching keywords to the right ad copy and landing page experience.

#### Phase 1: Trigger Event (Months Before First Search)

**What happens:** A life event creates the need.

Common triggers:
- **ADU/rental income desire** — housing costs rising, homeowner discovers they can legally rent their basement under HB 82/HB 398/SB 174
- **Aging parent moves in** — need a mother-in-law suite with separate entrance
- **Kids move home** — adult children need independent living space
- **Home office / business** — post-COVID need for a separate, ground-level workspace
- **Home sale preparation** — want to maximize sale price by adding livable square footage
- **Natural light frustration** — homeowner hates their dark basement and wants to transform it

**Search behavior at this stage:** Very broad — "how to make money from basement," "Utah basement apartment laws," "add value to home," "basement ideas"

#### Phase 2: Research & Education (2–6 Weeks)

**What happens:** Homeowner learns what a walkout basement is and whether their home qualifies.

**Searches:**
- "what is a walkout basement"
- "can you add a walkout to an existing basement"
- "walkout basement pros and cons"
- "walkout basement vs egress window"
- "walkout basement cost"
- "do I need a permit for a walkout basement in Utah"
- "walkout basement structural engineering"

**Behavior:** Reading blog posts, watching YouTube videos (Dansie Design Build dominates here), browsing before/after galleries. Average 3–5 sessions over 2–4 weeks.

**Opportunity:** Content marketing captures this audience. If budget-constrained, use RLSA (remarketing lists for search) to bid on these terms only for return visitors.

#### Phase 3: Contractor Evaluation (1–3 Weeks)

**What happens:** Homeowner has decided they want a walkout and is now comparing contractors.

**Searches:**
- "walkout basement contractor Utah"
- "walkout basement contractor near me"
- "best walkout basement contractor Salt Lake City"
- "walkout basement reviews Utah"
- "[competitor name] reviews"
- "walkout basement estimate"
- "walkout basement quote"

**Behavior:** Requesting 2–4 estimates, reading Google reviews, visiting contractor websites, calling during business hours. **This is where Google Ads ROI is highest.**

**Opportunity:** Bid aggressively on contractor/estimate/quote terms. Ad copy should emphasize free estimate, licensed/insured, and transparent pricing.

#### Phase 4: Decision & Commitment (1–2 Weeks)

**What happens:** Homeowner has received estimates and is making a final decision.

**Searches:**
- "walkout basement financing options"
- "[company name]" (brand search — checking you out)
- "questions to ask walkout basement contractor"
- "walkout basement contract"
- "walkout basement warranty"

**Behavior:** Final due diligence. May revisit your site 2–3 more times. Phone calls increase at this stage.

**Opportunity:** Remarketing display ads to form abandoners. Brand campaign (Phase 2 of campaign plan) captures branded search.

### Key Insight: The Research Cycle Is Long

Unlike emergency plumbing or HVAC, walkout basements are a **considered purchase** with a 1–3 month research cycle. This means:

1. **First-click attribution will undervalue Google Ads.** The first ad click plants the seed; the conversion may come via direct visit or organic search weeks later.
2. **Remarketing is essential.** Budget for RLSA at minimum.
3. **The lead magnet ("Utah Homeowner's Guide to Walkout Basements") is a critical mid-funnel asset.** Capture emails during Phase 2 to nurture through Phase 3.

## 9. Competitor Keyword Gaps

### What Competitors Are Bidding On (Inferred from Search Results)

| Competitor | Likely Keywords | Weakness |
|-----------|----------------|----------|
| **Wasatch Walkout Basements** | Brand terms, "walkout basement Utah," "walkout basement specialist" | Limited to exact-match domain SEO; unclear if running Google Ads |
| **Dansie Design Build** | "walkout basement cost," "basement walkout Herriman," cost-related long-tail | Focused on South Valley (Herriman/Riverton); less coverage of Utah County |
| **KFM Construction** | "concrete walkout basement contractor," "basement walkout Salt Lake City" | Generic concrete contractor — not positioned as walkout specialist |
| **Rivers Concrete** | "basement walkout installation," "walkout West Valley City" | Small, localized — West Valley/SLC only |
| **Angi / Thumbtack / HomeAdvisor** | All high-volume generic terms — "basement contractor," "basement remodel near me" | Pay-per-lead model; don't capture walkout-specific searches well |

### Gaps BaseScape Can Exploit

1. **ADU/Rental Income Keywords:** No competitor is bidding on "basement apartment entrance Utah," "ADU basement walkout," or "basement separate entrance Utah." This is a completely open keyword set with high intent.

2. **Cost Transparency Keywords:** Only Dansie publishes pricing content. Keywords like "walkout basement cost Utah" and "how much does a walkout basement cost" have minimal competition from other Utah contractors.

3. **City-Specific Long-Tail:** Few competitors are creating city-specific landing pages or ad groups. "Walkout basement contractor Sandy," "walkout basement Lehi," etc. are largely uncontested.

4. **Daylight Basement Capture:** No competitor is bidding on "daylight basement conversion" — these are confused searchers who actually want a walkout.

5. **Question Keywords:** "Do I need a permit for a walkout basement in Utah" and similar question queries have zero advertiser competition and can be captured with a blog post + RLSA.

## 10. Implementation Recommendations

### Phase 1 Priority (Launch at $300–500/mo)

**Focus on Tier 1 high-intent keywords only.** At this budget, every dollar must target someone ready to hire.

**Recommended ad groups to activate:**
1. Walkout Basement — Contractor (exact + phrase match)
2. Walkout Basement — Cost/Pricing (exact + phrase match)

**Estimated performance at $400/mo:**
- ~50–100 clicks/month at $4–8 avg CPC
- ~3–8 leads/month at 5–8% conversion rate
- ~$50–133 cost per lead

### Phase 2 Expansion ($500–800/mo)

Add:
3. Walkout Basement — Conversion/Construction
4. Basement Entrance / ADU (the gap opportunity)

### Phase 3 Scaling ($800–1,200/mo)

Add:
5. Question/Informational keywords (RLSA only)
6. City-specific ad groups for top-performing cities
7. Display remarketing to form abandoners

### Keyword Match Type Strategy

| Intent Level | Match Type | Rationale |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| High intent (contractor, near me) | Exact + Phrase | Protect budget, ensure relevance |
| Medium intent (cost, estimate) | Phrase | Capture variations while controlling spend |
| Low intent (questions, ideas) | Phrase (RLSA only) | Only bid on return visitors |

### Critical Day-1 Actions

1. Add ALL negative keywords from Section 7 before unpausing campaigns
2. Set location targeting to "People IN your targeted locations" (not "interested in")
3. Enable call tracking on all ads
4. Verify conversion tracking fires correctly (form_complete, phone_click, quick_callback_submit)
5. Review search terms report daily for first 2 weeks

### Ongoing Optimization

- **Weekly:** Review search terms report, add negatives, identify new keyword opportunities
- **Bi-weekly:** Adjust bids by ad group performance, pause underperformers
- **Monthly:** Evaluate CPA by ad group, reallocate budget to winners
- **Quarterly:** Expand keyword list based on search terms data, consider new ad groups
