## 3. Keyword Strategy

### Match Type Approach

**Phase 1 (weeks 1–8, building conversion data):**
- **Exact match** for city-specific high-intent terms (protect budget)
- **Phrase match** for "near me" and general contractor terms
- Build conversion data toward the 30+ conversions needed for smart bidding

**Phase 2 (once 30+ conversions accumulated):**
- Begin testing **broad match** on top-performing keywords paired with smart bidding
- Target allocation: 40% exact / 40% phrase / 20% broad
- Aggressive negative keyword management becomes even more critical with broad match

**Phase 3 (scaling):**
- Shift toward 30% exact / 30% phrase / 40% broad as smart bidding optimizes
- Broad match + smart bidding consistently outperforms phrase match when sufficient conversion data exists

## 5. Ad Copy

### RSA Best Practices Applied

Research shows:
- **Use 8–10 headlines** (not the max 15). Quality over quantity.
- **Pin only Position 1** (strongest headline). Let Google optimize the rest.
- **Use all 4 description slots** for variety.
- **Ad Strength rating has no proven correlation with actual performance.** Optimize for CTR and conversion rate, not "Excellent" ad strength.
- **CTA verbs that convert:** "Get," "Call," "Schedule," "Request" outperform "Submit" or "Learn More."

## 13. Quality Score Optimization

Quality Score is rated 1–10. A one-point increase reduces cost per conversion ~16%. Target: 7+ on all keywords.

### Three Components

|-----------|--------|-----------------|

| Ad Relevance | ~33% | Tightly themed ad groups (8–12 keywords with identical intent), mirror keyword in headline + description |
| Landing Page Experience | ~33% | Message match (LP headline = ad headline), fast load (<2.5s), mobile-first, Core Web Vitals passing, clear CTA |

### BaseScape Competitive Advantages
1. **Licensed General Contractor** — most retaining wall competitors are landscapers, not licensed GCs
2. **Structural engineering capability** — we engineer walls to soil pressure, surcharge loads, drainage specs
3. **Direct-to-homeowner** — no middleman markup (vs. aggregators)
4. **Niche focus** — dedicated landing pages and service pages vs. competitors listing 20+ services
5. **Permit handling end-to-end** — engineering, permits, inspections (many competitors leave permitting to homeowner)
6. **ADU/income angle (walkout)** — no competitor targeting ADU/apartment entrance keywords

### Keyword Gaps to Exploit

1. **ADU/rental income keywords** — zero competition, high intent (walkout campaign)
2. **Cost transparency** — only Dansie publishes pricing. Keywords like "walkout basement cost Utah" have minimal competition.
3. **City-specific long-tail** — few competitors bidding on "retaining wall contractor Sandy" or "walkout basement Lehi"
4. **Problem-based terms** — "hillside erosion control," "leaning retaining wall repair" — less aggregator competition
5. **Repair/replacement** — "retaining wall replacement contractor Utah" is wide open
6. **Daylight basement capture** — confused searchers who actually want a walkout
