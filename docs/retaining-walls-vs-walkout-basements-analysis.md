# Retaining Walls vs. Walkout Basements: Channel Strategy Analysis

> **Prepared:** 2026-04-03 | Steven Bunker, AchieveCMO
> **Client:** BaseScape (basescapeutah.com)
> **Market:** Wasatch Front, Utah
> **Purpose:** Inform budget allocation and channel strategy across Google Ads and Meta Ads

---

## Executive Summary

Retaining walls and walkout basements represent fundamentally different buyer journeys — and require different advertising strategies. The data from Google Keyword Planner (Utah, April 2026) reveals a stark contrast:

- **Retaining walls** are a known solution. People search for them. Google Ads captures existing demand efficiently at $4.39/click.
- **Walkout basements** are a hidden opportunity. Few homeowners know this service exists. Google Ads CPCs are 8x higher ($35/click) with 47x fewer clicks per dollar.

**Recommendation:** Lead with retaining walls on Google Ads (demand capture). Use Meta Ads with an offer-led strategy for walkout basements (demand creation). Test both services on Meta for side-by-side platform comparison data.

### Why Walkout Basement CPCs Are So High

The $35 average CPC seems counterintuitive for "Low" competition keywords. Several factors explain this:

1. **Real estate contamination** — "Walkout basement" appears in thousands of home listings. Google's algorithm prices ads against real estate advertisers with much higher budgets.
2. **Low volume + high bid range** — The top-of-page bid range is $7.13–$42.23. With very few searches, Google skews toward the high end.
3. **Broad match default** — Keyword Planner forecasts assume broad matching, which triggers on related real estate queries.
4. **No conversion history** — New accounts with no conversion data get worse CPC estimates. This should improve over time.

**Implication:** Even with exact match targeting and strong negative keyword lists, walkout basement CPCs on Google will likely remain $8–15+ per click. At $240/mo, that's only 16–30 clicks/month.

## 2. Buyer Journey Comparison

### Retaining Walls: Known Problem → Known Solution

```
Homeowner sees erosion / sloped yard / failing wall
    → "I need a retaining wall"
    → Google search: "retaining wall contractor Utah"
    → Clicks ad → Requests estimate
```

**Key characteristics:**
- **Visual, obvious problem** — homeowner can see the erosion, the leaning wall, the unusable yard
- **Well-understood solution** — "retaining wall" is common vocabulary
- **Seasonal urgency** — spring snowmelt reveals damage, creates urgency
- **Multiple price tiers** — $5K garden walls to $50K+ structural systems
- **Short research cycle** — 2–4 weeks from awareness to contractor search
- **Competitive landscape** — 10+ local contractors, but mostly landscapers (not licensed GCs)

### Walkout Basements: Hidden Opportunity → Unknown Solution

```
Homeowner has dark basement / wants rental income / needs separate entrance
    → Does NOT know "walkout basement" is an option
    → May search: "basement apartment entrance" or "add value to home"
    → Or never searches at all — needs to be shown the possibility
```

**Key characteristics:**
- **Problem exists but isn't labeled** — homeowner doesn't know what to search
- **Low market awareness** — most homeowners don't know you can add a walkout to an existing home
- **High emotional appeal** — transforms dark, unused space into bright, valuable living area
- **ADU/rental income angle** — Utah's HB 82/HB 398 makes basement apartments easier
- **Large ticket** — $25K–$40K projects with strong margins
- **Long research cycle** — 1–3 months from discovery to commitment
- **Very few competitors** — Dansie, Wasatch Walkouts, KFM, Rivers Concrete (4–5 total in Utah)
- **Visual transformation** — before/after content is extremely compelling

## 4. The Awareness Gap: Why Meta Ads for Walkout Basements

### The Core Problem

Most Utah homeowners don't know that:
1. You can add a walkout entrance to an **existing** basement
2. It's a construction project, not a remodel — it requires foundation cutting and excavation
3. It can add 20–30% to home value
4. It can create a legal rental unit (ADU) under Utah law
5. A licensed general contractor (not a landscaper) is who you hire

**You can't capture demand that doesn't exist yet.** Google Ads only works when someone searches. If the homeowner doesn't know the solution exists, they'll never search for it.

### Why Meta Ads Solve This

Facebook and Instagram let you **show** the transformation to homeowners who match the profile:

- **Homeowners** in Salt Lake County, Utah County, Davis County
- **Home value** $350K+ (basement likely exists, equity to invest)
- **Age 30–60** (established homeowners, not first-time buyers)
- **Interests:** home improvement, real estate investing, rental income, ADU, basement finishing
- **Lookalike audiences** from website visitors and form submitters (once data accumulates)

The visual formats (video, carousel, before/after) are perfectly suited to walkout basements because the transformation is dramatic and emotionally compelling.

### The Awareness Funnel

```
Meta Ad (before/after video or carousel)
    → "Wait, you can DO that to a basement?"
    → Click → Landing page education + offer
    → Lead magnet: "Utah Homeowner's Guide to Walkout Basements" (already built)
    → Email nurture → In-home consultation
    → OR: Direct form fill / phone call (high-intent viewers)
```

## 5. Offer-Led Strategy for Meta Ads

### Why Offer-Led?

On Facebook, you're interrupting someone's feed. They weren't searching for you. The ad must:
1. **Stop the scroll** — visual impact (before/after, transformation video)
2. **Create desire** — show what's possible
3. **Reduce risk** — make the next step easy and free
4. **Provide value** — give something before asking for something

A direct "Get a Free Estimate" CTA works on Google (they're already searching). On Facebook, you need a **value-first offer** that educates and qualifies simultaneously.

### Walkout Basement Offers (Ranked by Aggressiveness)

| Offer | Type | Friction | Expected CPL |
|-------|------|----------|-------------|
| **"Free Walkout Feasibility Assessment"** | In-home consultation | Medium | $30–60 |

| **"See If Your Basement Qualifies — Free 60-Second Quiz"** | Interactive quiz | Very low | $3–8 |
| **"Watch: Dark Basement → Bright Walkout in 3 Weeks"** (video) | Video view → retarget | None | $0.05–0.15 (per view) |
| **"$500 Off Your Walkout Project — Spring 2026"** | Discount offer | Medium | $20–40 |

**Recommended funnel:**
1. **Top of funnel:** Video ad showing transformation → build video view audience
2. **Middle of funnel:** Retarget video viewers with lead magnet or quiz offer
3. **Bottom of funnel:** Retarget lead magnet downloaders with "Free Feasibility Assessment" offer

## 10. Key Takeaways

1. **Retaining walls on Google Ads is a no-brainer.** $4.39 CPC, 35 clicks/month at $5/day, 2 estimated conversions. This should be the first campaign to launch and the first to scale.

2. **Walkout basements on Google Ads is a poor use of budget.** $35 CPC, <1 click/month at $5/day, 0 estimated conversions. The awareness gap means people aren't searching. You can't capture demand that doesn't exist.

3. **Meta Ads is the right channel for walkout basements.** The visual transformation, the ADU/rental income story, and the "most homeowners don't know this is possible" angle are built for feed-based advertising. An offer-led strategy (lead magnet → quiz → consultation) matches the longer research cycle.

4. **Test retaining walls on Meta too.** Before/after content, seasonal urgency, and problem-aware targeting (homeowners with hillside lots) could deliver a lower CPA than Google. You need side-by-side data to know.

5. **The offer matters more than the platform on Meta.** A "Free Walkout Feasibility Assessment" or "See If Your Basement Qualifies" quiz will dramatically outperform a generic "Get a Free Estimate" CTA. Give value before asking for commitment.

6. **Track beyond platform CPA.** The real metric is revenue per ad dollar. A walkout basement lead at $50 CPA that closes a $35K job is 10x more valuable than a retaining wall lead at $25 CPA that closes a $6K job. Track closed revenue by channel and service.
