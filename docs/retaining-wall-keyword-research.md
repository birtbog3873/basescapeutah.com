## 1. Market Context

### What Homeowners Actually Search

When homeowners need a retaining wall, their search journey typically follows this pattern:

1. **Problem awareness** -- They notice erosion, a sloping yard, a leaning/failing existing wall, or want to create usable yard space
2. **Solution research** -- They search for types of walls, materials, whether they need a permit, and costs
3. **Contractor search** -- They search for local contractors, get estimates, and compare options

The highest-value searches for BaseScape are in stages 2-3, where homeowners have identified a need and are evaluating solutions or actively seeking a contractor.

### Utah-Specific Demand Drivers

- **Wasatch Front topography:** Sloped lots along the Wasatch Range create natural demand for retaining walls
- **Clay-heavy soils:** Utah's clay soils shift with moisture and seasonal freeze-thaw cycles, driving wall failures and new construction needs
- **Spring snowmelt:** Annual snowmelt washes topsoil downhill, making spring the peak awareness period
- **4-foot permit threshold:** Utah municipalities require permits and engineering for walls over 4 feet, which pushes homeowners toward licensed contractors
- **Rapid development:** New subdivisions on hillside lots in Lehi, Herriman, Eagle Mountain, Saratoga Springs, and South Jordan create ongoing demand

### CPC Landscape for Retaining Wall Keywords
