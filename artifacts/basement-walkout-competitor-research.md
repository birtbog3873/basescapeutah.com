**Prepared by:** Steven Bunker, AchieveCMO
**Date:** March 20, 2026
**Market:** Salt Lake County, Utah County, Davis County

---

## Executive Summary

The basement walkout and egress window construction market along Utah's Wasatch Front is experiencing significant growth, driven by regulatory changes (HB 82), housing affordability pressures, and Utah's unique architectural landscape where nearly all homes have basements.

**Key Findings:**

- **Regulatory Catalyst:** HB 82 (2021) mandates allowance of Internal Accessory Dwelling Units (I-ADUs), requiring code-compliant exterior entrances and egress windows
- **Massive TAM:** Utah has some of the largest single-family homes in the nation, with 80-90% featuring basements — most unfinished
- **Fragmented Competition:** Market dominated by small local contractors and design-build firms, not national franchises
- **High Ticket:** Egress windows run $2,500-$6,800; full walkouts run $15,000-$40,000+
- **Severe Labor Shortage:** Only 72 available workers per 100 open construction jobs in Utah
- **Digital Gaps:** Most competitors have weak online presence — major opportunity for content marketing and transparent pricing
- **Seasonality:** Fall is optimal; winter exterior work is severely hampered by frozen soil and concrete curing challenges

## 2. Go-to-Market Strategy

GTM strategies are heavily skewed toward **direct-to-homeowner (B2C) sales**, augmented by strategic partnerships and digital marketing funnels.

### 2.1 Customer Acquisition Channels

- **Content Marketing and SEO:** Dominant acquisition channel. Companies like Dansie Design Build use hyper-local, long-form blog posts (e.g., "Footings and Rebar for a Basement Walkout Project in Herriman, Utah") to capture high-intent organic search traffic. Free cost calculators serve as highly effective lead magnets.
- **Video Marketing (YouTube):** High-ticket construction requires high trust. Dansie Design Build and Rock Top Construction use YouTube to show behind-the-scenes excavation, concrete cutting, and rebar installation. This demystifies the process and pre-sells competence.
- **Home Shows and Parades:** Utah's strong home showcase culture (Utah Valley Parade of Homes) generates top-of-funnel awareness for basement ADUs and walkouts.
- **Municipal and Financial Partnerships:** Utah Egress Windows taps directly into a government-subsidized lead stream as an approved contractor for Provo City's Egress Window Program. Other companies offer integrated financing to overcome the $30K+ capital barrier.

### 2.2 Sales Process

1. **Lead Capture:** Web forms offering "Free Estimates" or "No-Obligation Quotes"
2. **Initial Consultation & Site Visit:** Estimator assesses site access, soil conditions, foundation type, utility line locations (Blue Stakes)
3. **Engineering and Permitting (The Qualifier):** Structures deeper than 48 inches require structural engineering. Companies require a few hundred dollars for an engineer's stamp before a final fixed-price contract — this separates serious buyers from tire-kickers
4. **Contract and Scheduling:** Once permits are approved
5. **Execution:** Excavation, concrete cutting (door header), pouring footings/retaining walls, drainage installation, final backfill

### 2.3 Distribution Model

The majority sell **direct-to-homeowner**, though many also sub-contract for larger GCs building new homes.

## 5. Market Opportunity

### 5.1 Total Addressable Market (TAM)

Utah's unique housing stock creates one of the most lucrative markets in the U.S. for basement renovations:

- **Deep Foundations:** Utah's deep frost line means almost all single-family homes are built with basements
- **Largest Homes in America:** Provo/Orem metro ranks #1 nationally for mid-size metros — median home 3,393 sq ft
- **Massive Unfinished Inventory:** "All new construction in Utah County, unless a water table prohibits it, is built with unfinished basements"
- **Basement Prevalence:** Estimated 80-90% of single-family homes have basements

**TAM Formula:** Total SF Households on Wasatch Front x % with Basements (80-90%) x Conversion Rate (equity + desire for ADU/walkout)

With rising mortgage rates and housing shortages, homeowners are highly incentivized to "finish off the basements" rather than move.

### 5.2 The HB 82 Effect (Demand Catalyst)

Utah House Bill 82 (2021) stripped municipalities of their ability to ban internal ADUs in single-family zones. To legally rent a basement apartment, homeowners must meet:

1. Fire-rated separation between basement and main floor
2. Separate exterior entrance (highly preferred or mandated by local inspectors)
3. Egress windows in every bedroom
4. Dedicated off-street parking

This transformed basement walkouts from a luxury convenience into legally required infrastructure for income-generating properties.

### 5.3 Seasonality

| Season | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Winter (Dec-Feb)** | Exterior excavation severely hampered — frozen soil, expensive concrete curing measures needed |
| **Spring (Mar-May)** | Thawing ground and heavy rains cause mud/moisture delays |
| **Summer (Jun-Aug)** | Active season, but extreme heat can affect concrete |
| **Fall (Sep-Nov)** | Sweet spot — dry ground, stable weather, ideal concrete curing conditions |

## 8. Regulatory and Permitting Landscape

### 8.1 Building Code Requirements (IRC)

- Every basement bedroom/habitable space requires emergency escape and rescue opening (egress)
- Walkout doors must be operable from inside without keys or special knowledge
- Concrete cutting requires steel or wood header to reinforce foundation
- Retaining walls over 32 inches require guardrails; stairs require continuous handrails

### 8.2 Engineering Requirements

**Critical threshold:** Any retaining wall exceeding 48 inches requires a State of Utah Licensed Professional Engineer's wet-stamped plan. Engineers evaluate:
- Soil composition
- Groundwater levels
- Slope stability
- Surcharge loads (driveways, structures near wall)
- Steel rebar reinforcement requirements

### 8.3 Permit Process (by Municipality)

**Herriman (Salt Lake County):**
- Plan review: ~7-10 working days
- Permit fee: $176.94
- Required inspections: Footing, Foundation, Rough Electrical, Final

**Riverton (Salt Lake County):**
- Walkout/retaining wall over 4ft permit: $175.00

**Eagle Mountain (Utah County):**
- Sub-grade structures cannot encroach into required setbacks
- Cannot be located within an easement or front yard

### 8.4 Required Permits per Project

| Permit Type | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| Building Permit | Structural modifications, foundation cutting, framing |
| Electrical Permit | Exterior light and outlet by new walkout door |
| Plumbing Permit | Exterior drain at stairwell bottom, sump pump tie-in |

### 8.5 I-ADU Registration

- Salt Lake City limits ADU height/footprint, requires owner-occupancy of primary structure
- North Salt Lake requires certification of egress windows, strapped water heaters, basement stair handrails

## 9. Workforce and Delivery Model

### 9.1 The Utah Labor Shortage

- **72 available workers for every 100 open construction jobs**
- Utah's Department of Workforce Services projected need for 19,000+ new construction hires in 2024
- Total construction employment: ~105,000 workers, but demand vastly outpaces supply
- **Crew poaching is rampant:** Rival contractors offer signing bonuses and $3-$4/hr immediate wage increases to entire crews on active job sites
- Some firms issuing multiple raises per year to retain talent

### 9.2 Trades Involved in a Walkout Project

| Trade | Scope |
|-------|-------|
| Excavation & Grading | Heavy machinery, dirt removal, site grading |
| Concrete Cutting & Pouring | Foundation cutting, footings, retaining walls, stairs |
| Waterproofing & Drainage | French drains, sump pumps, exterior membranes |
| Carpentry & Framing | Headers, doors, structural reinforcement |
| Electrical | Exterior lighting, threshold, flashing |

### 9.3 Delivery Model Mix

- **Larger GCs** (Westar, Rock Top) handle concrete flatwork and excavation in-house
- **Smaller firms** rely heavily on subcontractors for specialized tasks
- **Quality control risk:** Subcontractor reliance introduces the #1 source of negative reviews (see Section 7)

## 10. Adjacent and Substitute Services

### 10.1 Egress Windows (Primary Substitute)

The most direct substitute. Satisfies IRC life-safety requirements at a fraction of the cost:
- **Cost:** $2,550-$6,800 (avg $4,250) vs. $25,000-$40,000+ for a full walkout
- **When chosen:** Code compliance for bedroom classification, natural light, budget constraints
- **When walkout wins:** Creating a physical entrance for ADU, mother-in-law suite, or home business

### 10.2 Basement Finishing Contractors (Adjacent)

Walkout construction is frequently bundled with comprehensive basement finishing. Design-build firms capturing both the walkout AND interior finish capture a much larger share of the homeowner's budget.

### 10.3 Foundation Repair & Waterproofing (Adjacent)

Companies like Ram Jack, Davenport Foundation Repair, and '58 Foundations compete for budgets when homeowners face hydrostatic pressure, settling, or water intrusion. Homeowners must choose between preventative waterproofing and cosmetic upgrades like walkouts.

## 13. Strategic Implications

### Competitive Advantages Available

1. **Content Marketing Moat:** The bar is low. Transparent pricing, educational blog content, and YouTube videos would immediately differentiate.
2. **Financing Integration:** Embedding financing options (Hearth or similar) reduces the $25K-$40K purchase barrier.
3. **Communication Excellence:** The #1 complaint pattern is communication failures — simply being responsive and transparent wins.
4. **Subcontractor Quality Control:** Rigorous sub management and site cleanliness would address the most common negative reviews.
5. **HB 82 Education:** Most homeowners don't fully understand the ADU opportunity — educating them creates demand.

### Barriers to Entry

1. **Specialized Heavy Machinery:** Excavation equipment requirements
2. **Structural Engineering Liability:** Altering home foundations carries significant risk
3. **Skilled Labor Shortage:** 72 workers per 100 jobs; crew retention is expensive
4. **Permitting Knowledge:** Municipality-specific requirements across 50+ cities
5. **Seasonality:** 3-4 month effective reduction in exterior work capacity

### Market Timing

The convergence of HB 82, housing affordability crisis, high home equity levels, and aging housing stock creates a sustained demand cycle. Contractors who master structural excavation, concrete flatwork, and municipal code compliance will find a highly lucrative market opportunity.
